This dish uses an Indonesian cuisine called Tempeh (Tempe in Indonesian spelling), which is sort of a fermented soybean cake. Tempeh are usually thinly cut and cooked to be crispy or to be cut in chunks and cooked for a soft texture. In this dish Jōichirō used the tempeh as a meat substitute. Tempeh have been known to be used as a substitute of meat due to the likenesses of texture and flavor. Tempeh is a staple in every Indonesian dining table.
